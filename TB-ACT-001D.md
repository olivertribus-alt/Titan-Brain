# TB-ACT-001D — Fault Injection and ROS 2 Jazzy E2E

## Status

Implemented as the final TB-ACT-001 verification slice. The deterministic
fault matrix runs against the dependency-free stop monitor and the ROS 2 test
suite exercises the same latch boundary through
`actuator_feedback_monitor_node`.

## Fault matrix

| Scenario | Injected condition | Expected result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Encoder drift / mechanical inertia | A moving sample remains present at the stop-budget boundary | `STOP_TIMEOUT` and `HARDWARE_FAULT_LATCH` |
| Frozen feedback | A sample timestamp exceeds the freshness budget | `STALE_FEEDBACK` and `HARDWARE_FAULT_LATCH` |
| Correlation desynchronisation | Feedback carries another `correlation_id` | `FEEDBACK_CORRELATION_MISMATCH` and latch |
| Sequence replay | A sequence is repeated or regresses | `FEEDBACK_SEQUENCE_REGRESSION` and latch |
| Sequence gap | A forward sequence jump is observed | `FEEDBACK_SEQUENCE_GAP` and latch |
| Non-finite measurement | `NaN` or infinity reaches the feedback boundary | `INVALID_FEEDBACK` and latch |
| Spurious movement | A fresh moving sample follows a stop acknowledgement | `SPURIOUS_MOVEMENT_AFTER_ACK` and latch |

Every case is generated by `core/actuator_fault_injection.py` and executed
against a fresh `StopAckMonitor`. The harness records the final state, reason,
and number of processed events, making the expected fail-closed response
explicit and replayable in unit tests.

## Latch and recovery contract

- Once a fault is latched, timer ticks, valid stopped feedback, new stop
  requests, and later motion cannot clear it.
- The monitor publishes the specific fault reason rather than collapsing it
  into a generic recovery state.
- Only an explicit, non-future `HardwareResetRequest` at or after the latch
  timestamp returns the monitor to `IDLE`.
- Acknowledgement is still monitored: any subsequent fresh movement is treated
  as uncommanded actuator motion and immediately becomes critical.

## ROS 2 E2E coverage

The Jazzy test gate covers startup/status publication, a normal stop
acknowledgement, invalid transport feedback, frozen feedback, and spurious
movement after acknowledgement. A latched result is published with
`critical=true` and `priority=255` on `/actuator/stop_acknowledgement` and
`/actuator/status`.

The local environment does not provide `/opt/ros/jazzy`; generated-message and
runtime ROS tests therefore run in the GitHub Actions ROS 2 Jazzy container.
